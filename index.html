Natural Disaster Safety Information

Blizzard Safety
  • Blizzards typically have fair warnings before their arrival.
  • Preemptively stored food and water that will last a few days in case you end up stranded.
  • If you live in an area prone to blizzards, make sure the machinery you rely on, such as your car or a heater, is well maintaned.
  • Keep your vehicleâ€™s gas tank full so you can leave right away in an emergency and to keep the fuel line from freezing.
  • Stock yourself with snow removal equipment well before the news appears
  • In the event that access to heat is scarce, limit you and your family to one room in the house. Block off any possible exits (door, windows, ect) with a towel, blanket or some form of heat insulator.
  • If possible, prepare hot drinks.
  • Keep a close eye out for symptoms of hypothermia on you or a loved one
Tornado Safety
  • If tornadoes are frequent in your area, have a designated shelter.
  • Shelters are best underground or in the lowest level of a building without windows.
  • If in on a high level, try to reach the center of the building in a hallway.
  • A tornado watch indicates there is a possibility of a tornado occuring.
  • A tornado warning indicates a present or approaching tornado threat.
Hurricane Safety
  • If possible, move to a second location outside of the hurricane area before it hits. If you are warned to evacuate, evacuate.
  • Always stay inside during a hurricane.
  • Expect the loss of power and take precautions.
  • Go to a higher ground or a shelter. Flood waters from hurricanes can reach massive heights and destroy the bottoms of homes
  • Stay away from standing water, it may be electrically charged
  • Do not drink tap water until deemed safe to do so
Tsunami Safety
  • Protect yourself from a likely earthquake. (see Earth Quake Safety)
  • Evacuate as soon as possible. There is no safety in range of a tsunami.
  • Get as inland and high ground as possible.
  • If you are in a boat, go out to sea.
Earthquake Safety
  • Earth quakes happen fast and with little warning.
  • Prepare yourself with food and water in case of service shutdowns in your area, if possible.
  • If you are in a car, pull over to an open area and stop, setting your parking break.
  • If you are outdoors, stay away from buildings and large objects.
  • If you are indoors, stay indoors and remain away from large objects or doorways. Take cover if possible.
  • If you were sleeping in a bed, cover your neck and head with a pillow and take cover
  • Wait for the shaking to stop completely
Flood Safety
  • Flash floods typically have a fair warning before arrival
  • Avoid parking in areas that are prone to flooding.
  • Tie down objects that are likely to be washed away.
  • Stay indoors, at at least ground level
  • Keep electronic devices out of range of flood waters and avoid flood water as it may be electrically charged
  • Don't drive or walk through flood waters if another option is available
